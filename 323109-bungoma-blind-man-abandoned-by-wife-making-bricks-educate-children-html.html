When a couple gets married, one of their wishes is to be able to live as per the vows they made at the alter including sticking by each other in both sickness and health.

However, that was not the case for one man in Bungoma county who not only lost his sight in 2015 but his wife too as a result.

Joseph Walueng'o from Tongaren constituency has, however, stayed focused on his life and even found a new line of business to fend for his children, NTV reported.

Walueng'o had been fighting an eye condition since his high school days in 1982 and eventually went blind in 2015.

The loss of his sight made him lose his wife to another man after life became unbearable for both of them.

READ ALSO: McDonald Mariga promises to get rid of police stations in Kibra if elected

"We had constant wrangles which left me weak and financially unstable so when my wife left she knew I would not survive because everything was hopeless. for me," he said"I asked myself how I would survive because despite losing my eyesight I still needed to move on," he added.

On realising that there was no job he could do, he decided to venture into brick making, a business that has seen his children through High school and university education.

According to his neighbours, his blindness encouraged him rather than discourage him although the brick market has become competitive making it difficult to market his products

"I started with a vision of burning 50, 000 bricks then I slowly started molding them," he said.

At first, it was difficult since it was trial and error for him until he eventually learned how to go about it.
