Mombasa: Armed Thugs Attack Police Commander, Rob Him of His Gun

June 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Mombasa police commander George Kingi was attacked as he was on his way home on the night of Monday, September 21
  • A tuk-tuk obstructed Kingi's personal car near Ummu Khulthum mosque prompting him to embark from the car
  • However, one thug pointed a gun at him as he was seeking answers from the armed men

Detectives in Mombasa county are probing an incident in which a senior police officer was left unarmed after losing his firearm to thugs.

Mombasa police commander George Kingi was on his way home on the night of Monday, September 21, when he was accosted by unknown men who robbed him of his rifle.

A police report on the night incident indicated a tuk-tuk obstructed Kingi's personal car near Ummu Khulthum mosque prompting him to embark from the car and went to enquire if there was any problem.

"He alighted from his car and went to inquire the intention of the occupants. One of them pointed a firearm at him while the other two searched him and in process, he was robbed off his official pistol make Jericho s/no: 45303132 loaded with 15 rounds of ammunition which he had holstered on his waist," the police said in the report.

The three then sped off towards the Likoni ferry.

Two thugs gunned down

The incident happened barely one week after a similar case involving police officers and thugs on the night of Friday, September 17.

The robbers were armed with a firearm and several rounds of ammunition which the detectives recovered after they were fatally shot.

According to the detectives, the thugs had planned attacks in the environs of Embakasi before the detectives caught up with them.

Upon noting they were being pursued, they opened fire at the detectives as they tried to scamper away from the law enforcers.

The officers returned the fire, killing two of them as the third managed to escape to the neighbouring Mukuru kwa Njenga settlements.

Following the operation, detectives recovered an Austrian made Glock pistol and 29 live rounds of 9 mm calibre from one of the fallen gangsters.

After their preliminary ballistic tests, the detectives noticed the same firearm had been used to commit various robberies in the city, including a cold blood murder in Mukuru Kwa Njenga slums.

The thugs were also linked to the series of robberies targeting mobile money lending shops within Nairobi’s Eastlands areas.
