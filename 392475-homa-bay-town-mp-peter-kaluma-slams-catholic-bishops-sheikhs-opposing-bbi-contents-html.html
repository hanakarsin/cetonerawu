Homa Bay Town Member of Parliament Peter Kaluma has called out a section of Church and Muslim leaders opposing the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) proposals.

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) legislator said the issues being raised by a section of Catholic bishops and Muslim sheikhs were misinformed.

Taking to his Twitter on Sunday, November 22, the lawmaker vehemently rebuked the men of God claiming their ignorance is why the constitution upholds separation of religion and state.

“Having read the misinformed rants from my Catholic Bishops and Muslim Sheikhs on the BBI, I now appreciate why our Constitution asserts that State and religion shall be separate. The ignorance our religious leaders spew over BBI and matters governance is nauseating,” said Kaluma.

Catholic bishops' reservations on BBI

The Kenya Conference of Catholic Bishops (KCCB) had voiced its concerns about the BBI report.

Through KCCB chairperson Philip Anyolo, the 23 bishops cautioned that the document, if passed in its current form, may create more problems than it will solve.

“To give the president the power to appoint the prime minister and two deputies risks consolidating power around the president thereby creating an imperial presidency. This amendment could be creating the same problem it is set out to solve,” Anyolo stated.

This position has, however, been countered, with BBI architects clarifying that the initiative does not seek to add any power to the president.

Instead, they have noted that the head of state will now have to share power with a prime minister and two deputy prime ministers, and will still be unable to fire his deputy at any point. Presidential orders and decisions will also still be subject to judicial review and quashing as is the case today.

On the issue of expansion of Parliament, the Catholic bishops observed that increasing the number of legislators will be a huge burden to the taxpayers presently struggling to support the current representatives.

“There is no need why we should have such a large number of legislators. We do not want more government but better government,” they stressed

Muslim leaders' concerns on BBI

Just like their Catholic counterparts, Muslim leaders asked Kenyans to reject the report because it will bring dictatorship in the country.

The Sheikhs insisted that the push for constitutional amendments through the BBI will claw back the gains made by the 2010 Constitution.

Speaking to the press on Sunday, November 22, the leaders who were led by Supreme Council of Kenya Muslims chairman Hassan Ole Naado advised President Uhuru Kenyatta and his handshake counterpart Raila Odinga to postpone BBI campaigns and focus on urgent issues such as COVID-19.

“The two leaders should focus on control of COVID-19 due to the rising cases of deaths and infections in the country and not constitutional amendments,” he said.

Previously, Kaluma had reaffirmed that the constitutional reforms process will not be stopped by COVID-19 as the disease will never stop the world.

“The world will never stop because of the coronavirus. USA and Tanzania are just done with national elections. Uganda is campaigning. Bring BBI referendum we vote,” he stated on Wednesday, November 18.
