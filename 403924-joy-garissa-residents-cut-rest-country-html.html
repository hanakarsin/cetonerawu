Joy at last for Garissa residents after construction of new communication mast

February 2024 Â· 3 minute read

- Residents of Hulugho, Garissa county, have a new lease of life following the construction of a new Safaricom communication mast in the area

- The mast was installed inside a police station and was launched by Ijara MP Sophia Abdi Noor

- The MP said the mast will help restore communication network in the areas around thus benefitting the locals whom she said "had been cut off from the rest of the country"

For a while, residents of Hulugho in Garissa county had felt disconnected from the rest of the country and the world due to a poor communication network.

They could not make calls during emergencies or enjoy mobile money transfer services like the rest of Kenyans due to frequent disruption of telecommunication services occasioned by the heightened security threats.

A number of them had contemplated relocating to the areas neighboring Hulugho.

"We lost loved ones as we could not make emergency calls to get taxis or ambulance to rush the sick to hospital," Abdi Mohamed, a local in Hulugho.

In the recent past, the Al Shabab militant group had been on record attacking the border town and as a result, destroying the existing communication masts.

Lack of communication masts greatly impeded easy communication with security apparatus thus delaying responses whenever there would be attacks such as the ones by the Al Shabab militants.

The area MP Sophia Abi Noor termed the new development as a "reprieve to the locals".

"The realization of this long-awaited dream, therefore, comes as a reprieve and the beginning of economic and social growth," the MP said as she expressed her gratitude to the station's officers for agreeing to host and protect the mast.

Following the installation of the new mast by Safaricom, the Hulugho residents are now in a celebratory mood and will start to enjoy telecommunication.

For purposes of security, the mast was constructed inside the Hulugho police station.

With the mast enabling internet connectivity, MP Noor was upbeat the residents will now be able to easily access government services such as renewal of driving licenses and also enjoy online commerce like online banking.

"The residents in the area are now able to send and receive money from their loved ones. People on other telecommunication providers are as well able to send money to or through Mpesa," the MP stated.

The Hulugho locality recently experienced a considerable mushrooming of Mpesa outlets, Sophia Noor noting the possibility of business activities booming in the area.

On matters of security, the police reported a notable alleviation of incidents of burglary and money theft thanks to the presence of digital wallets like Mpesa which have enhanced money security.

The residents remembered how they had to grapple with walking more than 20km to Ijara, the nearest town, to access services.
