Kenyan newspaper review for October 2020: 5 counties stare at fresh lockdown as COVID-19 cases spike

March 2024 · 4 minute read

The national dailies on Tuesday, October 20, focused on the Mashujaa Day celebrations set to be held at Gusii Stadium under strict COVID-19 rules.

The papers also widely focused on the release of the Building Bridges Initiatives ( BBI) that will most likely redefine the 2022 succession politics.

1. Daily Nation

The plan to recall other learners back to school is now in jeopardy following the rising COVID-19 positivity rate which now stands at 12%, Daily Nation reports.

Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe has not yet approved the plan to allow other students back in school owing to the surge of COVID-19 cases in the country, which could lead to tighter containment measures.

The Education Ministry blamed its health counterpart for the current limbo and failure to offer guidance on the fate of other learners.

The chairperson of the National Association of Parents Nicholas Maiya said parents will petition the government to shut down schools if any learner gets infected with the virus.

At the same time, five counties are also staring at a possible fresh lockdown to curb the spread of COVID-19.

A well-placed source in the COVID-19 National Emergency Response Committee said experts are convinced Nairobi, Turkana, Nakuru, Mombasa and Kericho counties must be placed under stricter measures to control the sharp rise of COVID-19 infections.

2. Taifa Leo

According to Taifa Leo, all eyes are on President Uhuru Kenyatta after parliament passed a bill that will lead to the nomination of a seven-member panel that will recruit Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) commissioners.

If the president assents to the bill, the Parliamentary Service Commission (PSC) will name four of the IEBC panelists, nominate one member from Law Society of Kenya (LSK) and two will be picked by the Inter-Religious Council of Kenya.

Interestingly, the members of the PSC are all allies of Uhuru and his handshake companion Raila Odinga, which could give them an edge over Deputy President William Ruto in the upcoming elections.

The members of PSC include Speaker Justin Muturi deputised by Naomi Shaban (Taveta), Adan Keynan ( Eldas), Beth Mugo (nominated), George Khaniri (Vihiga senator), Rachel Ameso (Makueni woman rep) and Wiper's Ben Momanyi, all allies of Uhuru and Raila.

3. The Standard

The newspaper examines the fate of Opposition leader Raila Odinga and Deputy President William Ruto once the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) is released.

The document, which insiders assured will be made public sooner than later, will redefine succession politics in Kenya with either Raila or Ruto set for a major political heartbreak if either of their bets on the BBI flops.

For the ODM leader, success of the BBI will give him the much-needed return to glory moment after losing out on presidential elections four times while for Ruto, it will give him the chance to bolt from the Jubilee government.

According to political analyst Herman Manyora, the failure of the handshake report will give Ruto's rivals shivers down their spine as it will prove they have a strong challenger while its success will be a complete political resurgence for Raila.

4. The Star

The Mashujaa Day celebrations will be held under strict compliance to COVID-19 rules such as the washing of hands, wearing of masks and social distancing, The Star reports.

Speaking during a media-briefing, Nyanza regional-coordinator Magu Mutundika said only 4,000 people will be allowed into Gusii Stadium in the invite-only event.

Mutundika said those not invited will be allowed to visit Kisii Sports Club where a big screen will be mounted for them to follow the event.

The fete in Kisii is the first public celebration that Kenya will have since the outbreak of COVID-19 in March 2020.

5. People Daily

According to the paper, Government Spokesperson Cyrus Oguna has asked schools and college students who were enrolled in the Kazi Mtaani programme to go back to school.

The colonel said many students were unwilling to return to their institutions after getting jobs under the initiative.

He said the jobs were only temporarily and further called on parents and school heads to ensure all students resume learning.
