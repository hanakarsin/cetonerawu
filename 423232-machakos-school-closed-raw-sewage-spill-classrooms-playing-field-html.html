Machakos School Closed after Raw Sewage Spills into Classrooms, Playing Field

July 2024 · 3 minute read

A private school in Syokimau, Machakos county, has been forced to close down temporarily after raw sewage spilt into classrooms and the institution's playing field.

Little Bees Kindergarten and Primary school administration said it was forced to close for three weeks due to the spill caused by the construction of the Nairobi Expressway.

A report by Citizen TV on Thursday, August 19, showed the construction of the expressway and the Syokimau Loneview access lane had caused the diversion of untreated sewage into the school.

"When the sewer is flashed, there is nowhere that the sewage goes to. Therefore it all floods into the school compound," said Lorna Cheptoo, the school proprietor.

According to her, the school is counting losses in millions as a result.

According to the report, the parliamentary committee on environment, through the intervention of the area Member of Parliament (MP), Patrick Makau, visited the school on Tuesday, August 17.

The committee, led by its chairman Francis Chachu demanded the China Roads and Bridge Corporation contractor to address the problem.

“Makau brought a petition to Parliament to urge the House to move with speed to ensure that this issue is sorted out and that’s why we are here today,” Chachu said."We also had a meeting with the stakeholders, relevant agencies and owner of the affected school to understand the problems and how they were being addressed.” he added.

A similar scenario was witnessed in Riruta Satelite in Nairobi's Dagoretti area, where the contractor of the Dumaini road has diverted the sewage water to neighbouring homes.

Complaints to the Kenya Urban Roads Authority (KURA) and the Nairobi Metropolitan Service (NMS) by the residents has not yielded much.

"It is going into the fourth month yet this sewage is still draining here. It comes from Ngotho road and recolates here," said Anne Mumbi, a resident in the area."Our children are not going to school and even us adults suffer stomach aches because of this sewage," she added.
