Sid Owen's biography

Sid Owen was born on 12th January 1972 in Chapel Market, London in England. He is 49 years old as of 2021. His parents are David (father) and Joan (mother). When Owen was little, his father went to prison, where he was serving a life sentence for armed robbery.

“I didn’t want to know him. I never had any respect for him because of how he was as a father and a husband.”

On the other hand, his mother succumbed to pancreatic cancer when the actor was only eight years old. He was raised among his four siblings, Mark, Darren, Martin, and Scott.

Owen dropped studying when he was 16, he attended Anna Scher Theatre School to sharpen his skills in the film industry.

While growing up, his favourite hobby was playing football. He took part in numerous celebrity matches in the process of practising his hobby. Since a young child to this date, he has been an avid supper of Arsenal.

Career

Sid began his career in the film industry in the early 1980s. He made his television debut in 1983, playing the role of Barry Thompson in Jury. The year that followed, he starred Patrick in Weekend House and Boy in Bottle Boys.

Sid Owen's film debut

In 1985, he made his film debut by starring Young Ned in Revolution. He starred alongside renowned actors such as Al Pacino and Donald Sutherland. In 1988, he landed his groundbreaking role as Ricky Butcher in EastEnders' the highly acclaimed BBC soap opera.

He gained popularity in the 1990s thanks to this character's relationship with Sam Mitchell and Bianca Jackson in the soap opera. He made a regular appearance on the soap until April 2000, when he opted to leave the show.

Music and songs

After departure, he decided to try out his luck in the music sector, precisely in pop music. He started by releasing a cover version of Michael Jackson's 1972 hit track, Good Thing Going. The jam was released on Mushroom Records and reached number 14 in the United Kingdom Singles Chart in June 2000.

This was followed up with a single that did not make it to the release. However, in 1995, he had released a Children in Need single with Patsy Palmer titled, You Better Believe It. The jam reached number 60 in the United Kingdom Singles Chart.

Sid Owen's return to soap opera

In 2002, he returned to EastEnders by first appearing in a spin-off episode titled Ricky and Bianca. The episode aired in May 2002, seeing him reunite with his ex-wife Bianca. He started making regular appearances later the same year. Unfortunately, his role was axed in 2004 due to the lack of storylines.
