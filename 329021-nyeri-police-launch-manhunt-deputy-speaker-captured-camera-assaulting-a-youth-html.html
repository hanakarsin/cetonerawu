Nyeri police launch manhunt for deputy speaker captured on camera assaulting a youth

March 2024 · 2 minute read

- Chaos erupted at Giakanja Boys Secondary School on Sunday as Kieleweke and Tanga Tanga teams clashed

- Nyeri Deputy Speaker Samuel Kariuki was among the main participant in the fracas

- Kariuki was caught on camera beating up one of the youths

- Police have issued an arrest warrant against him despite having been arrested and released Sunday

Police officers in Nyeri county have launched a manhunt for Deputy Speaker Samuel Kariuki who was captured in a viral video thoroughly beating up one of the youths.

The video was captured on Sunday, December 8, when youths allied to Kieleweke and Tanga Tanga teams clashed at Giakanja Boys Secondary School.

In the video, the deputy speaker who is also Ruring’u wad representative is seen beating up the youth using a wooden rod as the other locals cheer him on.

The lad runs away before falling down but the seemingly irate deputy speaker continues to pounce on him.

Central regional commissioner Wilfred Nyagwanga said an arrest warrant had been issued against Kariuki and all those who were involved in the fracas will be arrested and presented in court.

At least 10 youth armed with crude weapons were arrested during the chaos which erupted ahead of a fundraiser where Deputy President William Ruto was the chief guest.

Some of the young people were captured burning t-shirts written "Uhuru's Bedroom" while singing pro-Ruto slogans.

However, it was not the first time for such fracas given on August 6 a group of youths disrupted Inua Mama meeting that had been hosted by area woman representative Rahab Mukami.

In all the fracas, a constant factor who is often accused of orchestrating the chaos is Nyeri Town MP Ngunjiri Wambugu who has always maintained the region is a no-go zone for those engaging in premature 2022 politics.
