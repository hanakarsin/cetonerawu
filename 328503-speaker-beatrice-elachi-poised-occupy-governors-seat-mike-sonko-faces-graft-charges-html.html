Speaker Beatrice Elachi likely to assume Nairobi governorship as Sonko faces graft charges

May 2024 · 3 minute read

- Mike Sonko is accused of several corruption and money laundering crimes

- If previous trends are anything to go by, the prosecution would be seeking to have the governor barred from accessing his office

- Governors Waititu and Moses Lenolkulal remain unwanted guests in their county offices to date

- Nairobi County Assembly Speaker Beatrice Elachi may easily occupy the governor's seat for 60 days should the court block Sonko from office

The arrest and looming prosecution of Nairobi governor Mike Sonko has presented a legal conundrum at the helm of the city county leadership.

Sonko is facing a litany of corruption charges relating to money laundering, unlawful acquisition of public property and other forms of economic crimes and criminal conduct.

The city politician is among other governors whose names have been mentioned, some having been prosecuted over irregular procurement procedures involving county tenders amounting to millions of shillings.

Legal experts opine the imminent prosecution of Sonko could plunge the county into a leadership crisis similar to that of Kiambu and Samburu counties' governors.

But Nairobi's a bigger crisis as the governor does not have a deputy unlike the other two beleaguered leaders.

Kiambu county chief Ferdinand Waititu and his Samburu counterpart Moses Lenolkulal remained barred from accessing their offices after denying several graft-related charges pending hearing and conclusion of their matters.

Ordinarily, a deputy governor takes over the running of county affairs in case of a sitting governor's demise, resignation or absence.

The flamboyant, yet controversial Sonko has had to work without a deputy since January 2018 when his assistant, Polycarp Igathe, resigned.

To date, the former Makadara lawmaker is still shopping for the best bet.

Should the court bar Sonko from his office, county Speaker Beatrice Elachi will occupy the governor's seat on acting capacity for 60 days after which an election of a governor is conducted.

"If a vacancy occurs in the of office of county governor and that of deputy county governor, or if the deputy county governor is unable to act, the Speaker of the county assembly shall act as county governor,” reads Article 182 of the Constitution.

In the meantime, the Director of Public Prosecution Noordin Haji is looking to prosecute other suspects alongside Sonko.

They are former county secretary Peter Mbugua, head of supply chain management Patrick Mwagangi and members of the tender committee Samuel Mwangi, Edwin Kariuki , Edwin Murimi among others.
