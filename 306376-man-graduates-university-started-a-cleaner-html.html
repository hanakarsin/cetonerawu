Man graduates from same university where he started as a cleaner

March 2024 · 3 minute read

A young man on Monday, May 20, graduated with a nursing degree from New York University’s Langone Tisch, where he once worked as a janitor.

Frank Baez was a teenager when he first started working as a janitor at New York University's Langone Tisch Hospital, cleaning patient rooms, bathrooms and hallways.

The 29-year-old who moved to New York from the Dominican Republic with his mom at the age of 15 got his first job in housekeeping at the hospital because he wanted to support his family.

"I could barely speak English at the time when I started working at New York University," said Baez, ''Now I reflect on it and I feel very proud of how much I accomplished," he added.

Once he started working, he became intrigued with the medical field and applied for and got a job as a patient transporter, taking patients to and from their rooms for surgeries and tests.

He eventually left the job to finish his bachelor's degree at nearby Hunter College, becoming the first person in his family to graduate from college.

But Baez said he always knew he wanted to return to where he started - New York University.

"While working with the nurses, I realized I wanted to be one of them. I learned how much they advocate for their patients and the passion they have for their job," he said.

Baez was encouraged by the nurses he worked with to apply to the university's Rory Meyers College of Nursing.

He entered an accelerated program that allowed him to graduate with a nursing degree in just 15 months.

"Our program is extremely rigorous, Frank did not just kind of make to the program, barely getting through. He flew through it with flying colors," the director of simulation learning at the college of nursing, Natalya Pasklinsky, said.

Pasklinsky worked as a nurse in the same unit where Baez worked as a janitor while in high school and saw him being promoted to patient transporter.

"The way he interacted with patients, to me showed a lot of compassion. In my mind, he is a star. I think he is going to be a fantastic nurse," she said.

Baez has already set his next goal of becoming a critical care nurse in an intensive care unit.

"I was never an A student. I just studied a lot and worked a lot," he said. "Of course there were times I doubted myself, but then I felt that I wanted to do something more for myself, that I deserved better, that I wanted to continue to move forward and grow and go on with my life," he added.
