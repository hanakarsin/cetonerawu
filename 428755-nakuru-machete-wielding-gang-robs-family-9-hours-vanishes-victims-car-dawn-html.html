The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) detectives based in Nakuru's Bahati area are pursuing a six-man gang that held a family hostage in a night-long robbery.

In the Tuesday, October 5 incident, the machete-wielding gang reportedly stormed the house minutes before 9pm when the seven-member family watched television.

As reported by DCI, after inflicting life-threatening injuries on the man of the house, the marauding gang then tied up the wife, daughter and three grandchildren aged between 10 and three, alongside their househelp.

The hoodlums who were not in a hurry brazenly ransacked the entire house for over 9 hours, packing the four TV sets, four mobile phones, three gas cylinders, a HP laptop, fridge, mortar, bicycle, assorted clothing, among other valuables.

To further milk the helpless family to the bones, they transferred KSh 80,000 from the daughter's Mpesa account and another KSh 61,800 from her bank account.

Escaping

Satisfied with their kill, they loaded the items into the family's Nissan NV200 van, which they used to escape at around 5.45am.

When the family finally managed to free themselves, they booked the robbery report at Bahati Police Station, where detectives initiated a manhunt for the criminal gang immediately.

Hours of the scrupulous pursuit of the perpetrators led detectives to Nakuru's Tuinuane area, where the stolen car was found abandoned by the roadside.

The injured man was rushed to a nearby local hospital, where he is recuperating.

A chase for the criminals has been launched to recover the stolen valuables.

Two robbers gunned down

On the night of Friday, September 17, detectives attached to DCI gunned down two of three suspects of robbery.

The robbers were armed with a firearm and several rounds of ammunition which the detectives recovered after they were fatally shot.

According to the DCI, the thugs had planned attacks in the environs of Embakasi before the detectives caught up with them.

Upon noting they were being pursued, they opened fire at the detectives as they tried to scamper away from the law enforcers.
