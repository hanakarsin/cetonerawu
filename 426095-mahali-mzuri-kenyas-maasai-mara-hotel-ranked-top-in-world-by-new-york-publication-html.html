A Kenyan hotel owned by British billionaire Sir Richard Branson is the best in the world according to the latest ranking.

Mahali Mzuri, a 12-tented luxury safari camp located in the private Olare Motorogi Conservancy in the wider Maasai Mara ecosystem in Narok county, was recently feted during the Travel + Leisure Awards 2021.

The 2021 world's best awards survey was open for voting from January 11, through May 10, 2021, as destinations around the world were lifting COVID-19 restrictions.

"We are delighted to announce that Mahali Mzuri has been voted the #1 hotel in the world in the Travel + Leisure Awards 2021! We are thrilled to have won this prestigious award and want to say a huge thank you to everyone who voted for us - we are so grateful," the company stated.

The Virgin Limited Edition's property also scooped the number one spot in the best Safari Lodge category in the 2021 awards.

The luxury facility can cater for up to 24 guests in the camp.

Every year, Travel + Leisure asks readers to weigh in on travel experiences around the globe to share their opinions on the top hotels, resorts, cities, islands or airlines.

Hotel guest compensated

Meanwhile, a court in Mombasa directed Sarova Whitesands Beach Resort and Spa to pay a family whose child drowned in the hotel's swimming pool KSh 2 million.

In the incident which occurred in 2018, the family had held a birthday party at the facility but it turned tragic when their nine-year-old boy drowned while swimming with other children.

The boy was rescued, rushed to a hospital in Nyali but succumbed while he was being resuscitated.
