Masakatalafu: Meaning Behind Chicken Naming in the Luhya Community

  • The Luhya people are known for rearing chicken and are so passionate about the practice
  • Due to the strong ties that exist between them and their chickens, they have given the birds names according to their physical features and existing myths
  • Etwaya (rooster) is the biggest and head of the family, while Enywinywi (chick) is the smallest of all

The Luhya community in Western Kenya is known for rearing chicken and has an insatiable appetite for its delicacy.

Chicken in Western Kenya

In most homesteads among the Abaluhya, you will not miss spotting one or two chickens roaming around the compound.

They cherish and passionately care for chickens; some families share the same house with domestic birds for security.

Due to the strong ties and bond between the Luhya people and their chickens, the birds have been given names according to their physical features like colour and pattern of feathers, picks, body size, habits, gender, etc.

Some are associated with strange myths such as luck, disaster, and curses.

According to Mzee Kefa Juma, there are over 16 names given to chicken.

Naming according to age and body size

1. Etwaya

This refers to a mature rooster. It is the father of other chickens on the homestead and is always at the forefront of defending the flock from any attack.

"Etwaya is very important in a homestead, it is like a father. It is needed for mating and it always protects the rest of the flock from external attacks." It is the one that wakes up everybody in the morning including the owner of home and leads other chicken back home in the evening," said Mzee Kefa.

Etwaya is rarely sacrificed for a meal; it is often the last option for the family. It is likened to a bull.

2. Esubeni

This is a young rooster who is still learning how to protect the rest and necessitate the process of mating.

It is often sacrificed for a meal during family events or given to a young male visitor, mostly a relative.

3. Esitakho

This is a hen that has attained maturity and the age of mating and laying eggs. Esitakho often produces a unique sound that naturally attracts the rooster.

"If you do not have a cock then it is important that you look for one before it migrates to the neighbourhood because you may never find it again. That is why I said Etwaya is very important in a home," explained Juma.

4. Omusenya

This refers to a hen that is older than Esitakho. In most cases, one that has previously laid eggs and hatched.

5. Enywinywi

This is the smallest of all. It refers to a chick.

Naming according to the colour and pattern of feathers

6. Enyange

Enyange refers to a white-feathered chicken. It is often associated with peace, luck, and success.

7. Enjaayi

This is a red-feathered chicken. It is the most common among families in Western Kenya.

8. Masakalafu

Its feathers spread across the body unevenly and often have various colours, like black and white. It is also known as Embukulu.

9. Kingetayi

Also known as Nalukosi. It has no feathers around the neck.

10. Enjika

Sometimes referred to as Nalukendo. It has very few, or sometimes no, feathers at all.

This is associated with bad omens and is never kept at home.
